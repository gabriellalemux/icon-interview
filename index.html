Real Solutions
01
Owning a Small Project
On the NDMC project, I managed the full scope independently — drawings, consultant coordination, federal review, and permitting support. It was the first time I had to track every piece of the project myself. Taught me how to stay organized under a lot of moving parts.
Project Ownership · Federal · Documentation
02
Building Envelope Details
At Little Elm HS, I worked through window-to-wall conditions at a large scale — coordinating with the envelope consultant and structural engineer to resolve flashing, thermal bridging, and framing conflicts before they became field issues. A lot of back and forth, but worth it.
Detailing · Coordination · Constructability
03
Consultant Coordination
Managing a large K–12 CD set means tracking a lot of consultants at once — MEP, structural, civil, geotechnical. I set up shared issue logs, led coordination check-ins, and made sure the Revit model stayed aligned with what the consultants were issuing. Not glamorous, but it keeps projects from falling apart.
BIM · Multi-Discipline · Organization

07 / The Future I Want to Build
Construction is
changing. Fast.
◻
Robotics & Fabrication
Watching Titan print structural walls faster than a traditional crew can frame them is genuinely exciting. The idea that a drawing can become a building with less labor, less waste, and more precision — that's the direction I want to work in.
⬡
Intelligent Workflows
BuildOS translating design intent into toolpaths and synchronizing material flow in real time — that's exactly the kind of system I want to understand and contribute to. Automated, precise, and team-coordinated.
◈
Resilient Infrastructure
ICON's wall system — printed concrete, spray foam, fire and mold resistance — solves real performance problems. Coming from K–12 and civic work where durability and lifecycle cost matter, I find that kind of technical focus genuinely interesting.
